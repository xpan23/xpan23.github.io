Projects

In my journey of Data Science, I threw down the gauntlet to every complex dataset I met. Here are some interesting ones I would like to share with you.

Air Quality Index Prediction with Spark ML and H2O

For this group project, we used Apache Spark on AWS EMR notebooks to build data processing ETL and distributed machine learning models.

Modeling on the 4GB Air Quality Index and Weather data, we managed to predict the AQI of LA county with a 13 MAE (300 based). The graph below shows how we got such a low MAE.

California Housing Price Prediction

We built SARIMA, SARIMAX and VAR models with Statsmodels and Pmdarima to predict the monthly median California housing price. Surprisingly, the winner is a simple SARIMA model, it achieved a RMSE of $2,511.58.

The graph below shows the difference of our predicted price and the actual price. It's tiny!

PUBG Finish Placement Prediction

Can we predict the finish placement of a PUBG game from the data in the game? Using the Scikit-learn Pipeline function, we can automate and combine the data preprocessing, model comparison and tuning into one workflow.

We also discussed the best strategy for the game by analysing permutation feature importance of the Random Forest model that achieved the best MedAE. Obviously, the best killer wins:)

Uber vs Lyft Pricing

Which app should we open when we need to grab a cab, Uber or Lyft? To answer this question, I explored a real-time queried price dataset with Pandas and used ggplot2 for visualization.

The most interesting finding was showed in the graph: Uber Pool is almost at the same price with a normal Lyft during rush hours, and Lyft Shared is much less expensive. So in rush hours, definitely go for Lyft Shared instead of Uber Pool.
